Why do we boil food?

Boiling is used to enhance the texture of starchy foods and tougher proteins, making them more edible. It also revives grains, dried pasta, and dried legumes, making them soft and tender.

How do you keep food safe?

How do you keep food safe? In every step of food preparation, follow the four guidelines to keep food safe: Clean—Wash hands and surfaces often. Separate—Don't cross-contaminate. Cook—Cook to proper temperatures, checking with a food thermometer. Chill—Refrigerate promptly

What temperature is food safe?

Note: There are three important temperatures to remember when cooking meat or eggs at home: Eggs and all ground meats must be cooked to 160°F; poultry and fowl to 165°F; and fresh meat steaks, chops and roasts to 145°F
